6. CONCLUSIONS FOR PT_COLOR ARTICLE
====================================================================

A. WHAT V4 NEURAL DATA CONFIRMS:
   - PT's luminance/chromaticity split (p=2 vs {3,5,7}) is echoed
     in V4 cortical responses (luminance 2.57x stronger)
   - The L-M channel weight in V4 matches gamma_3/(gamma_3+gamma_5+gamma_7)
     to 3.2% — a prediction from s=1/2 with 0 adjusted parameters
   - Low-contrast V4 responses correlate with Fisher distance on
     the SCS simplex (r=0.517 at 30% contrast)

B. WHAT V4 DATA DOES NOT DO:
   - Does NOT replace CAM02 in the hybrid model (0.675 vs 0.824)
   - The S channel is underrepresented in our functional ROI
   - 9 DKL directions cannot interpolate to arbitrary COMBVD colors

C. PT INTERPRETATION:
   - V4 color processing = Fisher metric on the simplex Delta^2
   - The gamma_p weights (0.808:0.696:0.595) set the relative
     sensitivity of the 3 chromatic channels
   - Luminance (p=2) and chromaticity ({3,5,7}) are processed
     in parallel, with w_lum = 3/4 (from N_active/(N_active+1))
   - The V4 data supports the SCS architecture but shows that
     additional cortical processing (adaptation, gain control,
     lateral inhibition) is needed beyond the Fisher geodesic

D. NEXT STEPS:
   - Use proper retinotopic mapping (task-meridianmapper) to
     define true V4 ROI (instead of functional proxy)
   - GLM analysis with motion correction for better BOLD estimates
   - Use V4 tuning curve to build a continuous color model
   - Test with human fMRI data (not just macaque)
